Conch Fritters

Description:

A Caribbean classic. Finely diced queen conch is mixed with seasoned batter, herbs, and spices, then deep-fried to a golden brown. Served with a zesty dipping sauce for a taste of the islands.

Ingredients

  • Conch
  • all-purpose flour
  • Milk
  • Eggs
  • Onions
  • Bell Peppers
  • Olive Oil

Instructions:

  1. If using fresh conch, rinse it thoroughly under cold water to remove any sand. The meat must be tenderized, often by pounding it with a meat mallet or rolling pin until slightly flattened.
  2. Finely chop or mince the tenderized conch meat into small pieces. You can use a food processor for this step, but pulse it carefully to avoid a paste.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper (if using).
  4. Stir in the chopped conch meat, onion, bell pepper, celery, garlic, and fresh herbs.
  5. In a separate smaller bowl, whisk together the beaten eggs and milk (or buttermilk/beer).
  6. Gradually pour the wet mixture into the conch mixture, stirring until everything is just combined. The batter should be thick and scoopable, not runny. Add a little more flour if it's too thin or a splash more milk if it's too thick.
  7. In a deep fryer or a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat about 2 inches of vegetable oil over medium-high heat until it reaches 350°F (175°C). Use a deep-fry thermometer to ensure the correct temperature.
  8. Carefully drop heaping spoonfuls of the batter into the hot oil, working in small batches to avoid overcrowding the pan and dropping the oil temperature.
  9. Fry the fritters for about 3-4 minutes, turning them occasionally (or once) until they are a rich golden brown color and cooked through.
  10. Use a slotted spoon to remove the cooked fritters from the oil and transfer them to a plate lined with paper towels to drain.
